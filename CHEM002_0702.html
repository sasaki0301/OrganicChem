Phenols and Naming Phenols

Phenols are aromatic compounds containing a hydroxyl (–OH) group directly attached to a benzene ring. Although they resemble alcohols, phenols behave differently due to the aromatic ring.

1. What Is a Phenol?

A phenol is an aromatic compound in which a hydroxyl group (–OH) is bonded directly to a carbon atom of a benzene ring.

Phenol: a benzene ring with an –OH group attached directly.

This direct attachment is important. If the –OH group is attached to a side chain instead of the ring, the compound is not a phenol.

Activity 1: Phenol or Not?

Which of the following is a phenol?

  • A) C₆H₅–OH
  • B) C₆H₅–CH₂OH
A is a phenol because the –OH is directly bonded to the aromatic ring.
B is an alcohol (benzyl alcohol), not a phenol.

2. Why Phenols Are Different from Alcohols

Phenols are structurally similar to alcohols but have distinct properties because the –OH group interacts with the aromatic π-electron system.

  • The O–H bond in phenols is more acidic than in alcohols.
  • The aromatic ring stabilizes the phenoxide ion.
  • Phenols often participate in substitution reactions on the ring.

Activity 2: Concept Check

Why is phenol more acidic than ethanol?

The negative charge formed after losing H⁺ can be delocalized over the aromatic ring in phenol, stabilizing the ion. Ethanol lacks this stabilization.

3. Naming Phenols: Basic Rules

When naming phenols, the benzene ring is the parent structure and the –OH group has priority.

Key Rules

  • The parent name is phenol, not benzene.
  • The –OH group is automatically at position 1.
  • Other substituents are numbered relative to the –OH group.
  • Substituents are listed alphabetically.

In phenols, the –OH group defines carbon 1.

Activity 3: Simple Naming

What is the correct name for a phenol with a methyl group at carbon 3?

3-methylphenol

4. Common Names: ortho, meta, para

In disubstituted phenols, the relative positions of substituents are sometimes described using:

  • ortho (o-): 1,2-position
  • meta (m-): 1,3-position
  • para (p-): 1,4-position

Substituents are listed alphabetically, and numbering starts from the –OH group.

Key Terms

  • Phenol: Aromatic compound with –OH directly attached to the ring.
  • Hydroxyl group: –OH functional group.
  • Phenyl group: Benzene ring acting as a substituent (C₆H₅–).
  • ortho (o-): 1,2-substitution pattern.
  • meta (m-): 1,3-substitution pattern.
  • para (p-): 1,4-substitution pattern.
