Cycloalkenes

Cycloalkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons that contain both a carbon ring and at least one carbon–carbon double bond.

1. What Are Cycloalkenes?

Cycloalkenes are hydrocarbons that:

  • Contain a ring structure
  • Have at least one C=C double bond
  • Follow the general formula CnH2n−2 (for one ring + one double bond)

Cyclohexene — a six-membered ring containing one double bond.

Activity 1: Identify the Class

Is cyclopentene an alkane, alkene, cycloalkane, or cycloalkene?

Cycloalkene.
It contains a ring (cyclo-) and a C=C double bond (-ene).

2. Structure and Bonding in Cycloalkenes

In cycloalkenes:

  • The carbons in the double bond are sp² hybridized
  • The ring restricts rotation around the double bond
  • This restriction allows geometric isomerism

Activity 2: Hybridization Check

What is the hybridization of the carbons involved in the double bond of cyclohexene?

sp² hybridized.
Each carbon forms three sigma bonds and one pi bond.

3. Naming Cycloalkenes (Basics)

Naming cycloalkenes follows these rules:

  1. The ring is the parent structure
  2. Number the ring starting at the double bond
  3. The double bond must receive carbons 1 and 2
  4. Substituents get the lowest possible numbers

Activity 3: Name the Compound

Name a six-membered ring containing one double bond and no substituents.

Cyclohexene

4. Cycloalkenes vs Alkenes

  • Alkenes: open-chain molecules with a C=C bond
  • Cycloalkenes: ring structures with a C=C bond
  • Cycloalkenes show cis–trans isomerism more naturally

Activity 4: True or False

Cycloalkenes can freely rotate around the C=C bond.

False.
Rotation around a double bond is restricted.

Key Terms

  • Cycloalkene: A cyclic hydrocarbon containing a C=C double bond
  • Unsaturated: Contains one or more multiple bonds
  • sp² hybridization: Orbital arrangement around double-bonded carbons
  • Geometric isomerism: Different spatial arrangements due to restricted rotation
