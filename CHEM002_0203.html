Hybridization, Sigma and Pi Bonds

This lesson explains how atomic orbitals mix (hybridize) to form new orbitals suitable for bonding, and how σ (sigma) and π (pi) bonds form from these overlaps.

1. What is hybridization?

Hybridization is the process by which atomic orbitals combine to form new, equivalent hybrid orbitals.
These orbitals explain the observed shapes and bond angles of molecules that cannot be predicted by simple s and p orbital models.

For example, carbon’s 2s and 2p orbitals mix to form four sp³ hybrid orbitals — explaining the tetrahedral geometry in methane (CH₄).

sp³ hybridization forms four equivalent orbitals arranged tetrahedrally (≈109.5°).

Activity 1: Think About Carbon

The ground-state electron configuration of carbon is 1s² 2s² 2p². How can it form four identical bonds in CH₄?

One 2s electron is promoted to the empty 2p orbital, giving four unpaired electrons.
The 2s and three 2p orbitals mix, forming four equivalent sp³ hybrid orbitals that each overlap with a hydrogen 1s orbital to form four σ bonds.

2. Types of hybridization

Hybridization depends on the number of regions of electron density (bonding or lone pairs) around the central atom:

  • sp – 2 regions → linear geometry (180°)
  • sp² – 3 regions → trigonal planar (120°)
  • sp³ – 4 regions → tetrahedral (109.5°)

Activity 2: Identify Hybridization

Determine the hybridization of the central atom in CO₂, BF₃, and CH₄.

CO₂ → sp (linear);
BF₃ → sp² (trigonal planar);
CH₄ → sp³ (tetrahedral).

3. Sigma (σ) and Pi (π) Bonds

Bonds form from orbital overlap: Sigma (σ) bonds are the first bonds formed by head-on overlap along the internuclear axis. Pi (π) bonds form from side-by-side overlap of unhybridized p orbitals.

σ bonds form from direct overlap; π bonds form from parallel p-orbital overlap above and below the σ bond.

Activity 3: Double Bonds and Triple Bonds

In ethene (C₂H₄), how many sigma and pi bonds are present? What about ethyne (C₂H₂)?

Ethene (C₂H₄): 1 σ bond between carbons + 1 π bond (double bond = 1σ + 1π).
Ethyne (C₂H₂): 1 σ bond + 2 π bonds (triple bond = 1σ + 2π).
