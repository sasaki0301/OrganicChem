Alkynes and Naming Alkynes

In this lesson, you will learn what alkynes are, how their triple bonds affect structure and reactivity, and how to name alkynes correctly using IUPAC rules.

1. What Are Alkynes?

Alkynes are hydrocarbons that contain at least one carbon–carbon triple bond (C≡C).

  • General formula (acyclic): CnH2n−2
  • They are unsaturated hydrocarbons
  • Triple bond = 1 σ bond + 2 π bonds

Ethyne (acetylene) — the simplest alkyne.

Activity 1: Identify the Hydrocarbon

Which feature identifies a molecule as an alkyne?

Presence of a carbon–carbon triple bond (C≡C).

2. Structure and Geometry of Alkynes

In alkynes, the carbons involved in the triple bond are sp-hybridized. This leads to a linear geometry.

  • Bond angle ≈ 180°
  • Very strong and short C≡C bond
  • Electrons are tightly held along the bond axis

Linear geometry around the C≡C triple bond.

Activity 2: Geometry Check

Why are alkynes linear rather than bent?

Because the carbons are sp-hybridized, producing two orbitals that orient 180° apart.

3. Naming Alkynes: Parent Chain

The naming of alkynes follows IUPAC rules similar to alkenes, but with the suffix -yne.

Step 1: Choose the Parent Chain

  • Longest chain that contains the triple bond
  • Parent name depends on number of carbons

Activity 3: Choose the Parent

Does the parent chain have to include the triple bond?

Yes. The parent chain must always include the triple bond.

4. Numbering the Triple Bond

Number the parent chain so that the triple bond gets the lowest possible number.

  • The number indicates where the triple bond starts
  • Double and triple bonds take priority over substituents

Activity 4: Numbering Practice

Which end should you number from to name an alkyne?

From the end that gives the triple bond the lowest number.

5. Full Naming Examples

Example 1

Parent chain: 4 carbons → but-
Triple bond at carbon 1 → but-1-yne

Example 2

Parent chain: 6 carbons → hex-
Triple bond starts at carbon 2 → hex-2-yne

6. Practice: Try It Yourself

Name a 7-carbon chain with a triple bond starting at carbon 3.

hept-3-yne

What suffix do all alkynes use?

The suffix -yne.

Key Terms

  • Alkyne: Hydrocarbon with at least one C≡C bond
  • Triple bond: One σ and two π bonds
  • sp hybridization: Orbital mixing that produces linear geometry
  • -yne: IUPAC suffix for alkynes
  • Unsaturated: Contains double or triple bonds
