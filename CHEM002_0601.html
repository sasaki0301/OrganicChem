Alcohols and Naming Alcohols

Alcohols are one of the most important functional groups in organic chemistry. They appear in fuels, medicines, disinfectants, beverages, and biological molecules.

1. What Is an Alcohol?

An alcohol is an organic compound that contains a hydroxyl group:

–OH

The hydroxyl group consists of an oxygen atom bonded to hydrogen and attached to a carbon atom.

Ethanol: a simple alcohol with one –OH group.

  • The carbon bonded to –OH must be sp³ hybridized.
  • Alcohols are different from phenols and carboxylic acids.
  • The –OH group strongly affects polarity and boiling point.

Activity 1: Identify the Alcohol

Which of the following must be present for a compound to be an alcohol?

  • A) A carbon–carbon double bond
  • B) An –OH group bonded to carbon
  • C) A carbonyl group (C=O)
  • D) A benzene ring
B — Alcohols contain a hydroxyl (–OH) group attached to a carbon atom.

2. Classification of Alcohols

Alcohols are classified based on how many carbon atoms are attached to the carbon bearing the –OH group.

  • Primary (1°): –OH carbon attached to 1 other carbon
  • Secondary (2°): –OH carbon attached to 2 carbons
  • Tertiary (3°): –OH carbon attached to 3 carbons

3. Naming Alcohols: Basic IUPAC Rules

Naming alcohols follows IUPAC rules similar to alkanes, with one key change:

Replace the -e ending of the parent alkane with -ol

Step-by-Step Overview

  1. Find the longest carbon chain containing the –OH group.
  2. Number the chain so the –OH gets the lowest possible number.
  3. Name the parent chain and replace -e with -ol.
  4. Indicate the position of the –OH group.

4. Naming Alcohols with Position Numbers

When the –OH group is not on the end of the chain, its position must be specified.

2-propanol: –OH group on carbon 2.

5. Alcohols with Substituents

If substituents are present:

  • Number the chain so the –OH gets the lowest number.
  • Name and number substituents as usual.
  • List substituents alphabetically.

Key Terms

  • Alcohol: Organic compound containing an –OH group.
  • Hydroxyl group: –OH functional group.
  • Primary alcohol: –OH carbon bonded to one carbon.
  • Secondary alcohol: –OH carbon bonded to two carbons.
  • Tertiary alcohol: –OH carbon bonded to three carbons.
  • -ol suffix: Indicates an alcohol in IUPAC naming.
