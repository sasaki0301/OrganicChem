Lesson 3: Applications of Organic Chemistry in Daily Life

Organic chemistry is deeply connected to everyday living. From the food we consume to the medicines we take and the materials we rely on, organic compounds shape our modern world.

1. Medicine and Pharmaceuticals

Many medicines are organic molecules. Aspirin, paracetamol, antibiotics, and countless modern drugs are products of organic synthesis. Organic chemistry helps design safer, more effective medications by understanding how molecules interact within the body.

Aspirin (C₉H₈O₄) – one of the most commonly used organic medicines.

Activity: Why are drugs organic?

Explain why most medicines are organic molecules rather than inorganic ions.

Because the body is made of organic molecules (proteins, lipids, DNA), medicines must also be organic to interact correctly with biological systems.

2. Fuels and Energy

Organic compounds such as methane, propane, gasoline, and kerosene power transportation, heating, and electricity generation. Even renewable fuels like ethanol and biodiesel come from organic agricultural sources.

Combustion of hydrocarbons releases energy used for cooking, transport, and electricity.

3. Food and Nutrition

Our essential nutrients — carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins — are all organic compounds. Organic chemistry explains metabolism, digestion, and how nutrients support life processes.

Proteins: complex organic molecules folded into functional shapes.

4. Polymers and Plastics

Organic chemistry is central to creating plastics, rubber, synthetic fibers, adhesives, paints, and countless household materials. Polymers are long chains of repeating organic units.

Polyethylene – one of the most widely used plastics.

5. Agriculture and the Environment

Fertilizers, pesticides, herbicides, and even plant hormones involve organic chemistry. Environmentally friendly organic compounds are now being developed to reduce pollution and create biodegradable materials.

Organic agricultural chemicals improve productivity and sustainability.

Summary

  • Organic chemistry explains the structure and behavior of carbon-based compounds.
  • It is essential to medicine, energy, food, materials, and the environment.
  • Organic compounds make up nutrients, fuels, plastics, and many parts of modern technology.
