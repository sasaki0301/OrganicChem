Formal Charges

Formal charge is a bookkeeping tool to assign charges to atoms in Lewis structures. It helps choose the best Lewis structure and predict where charges sit in molecules or ions.

1. Definition & Formula

Formal charge (FC) is the hypothetical charge an atom would have if all bonding electrons were shared equally. Use this formula:

FC = V − (N + B/2)

  • V = valence electrons in the neutral atom
  • N = nonbonding electrons (lone-pair electrons on that atom)
  • B = bonding electrons around that atom (each bond = 2 electrons)

Example: Formal charge on oxygen in H2O

Calculate FC for the oxygen atom in H–O–H.

V = 6; N = 4 (two lone pairs → 4 electrons); B = 4 (two O–H bonds → 4 electrons).
FC = 6 − (4 + 4/2) = 6 − (4 + 2) = 0. Oxygen formal charge = 0.

2. Why formal charge matters

Use formal charges to:

  • Compare alternative Lewis structures (pick the one with smaller magnitude formal charges).
  • Ensure the sum of formal charges equals the overall molecular/ionic charge.
  • Predict likely sites for attack: negative FC often indicates nucleophilic sites; positive FC indicates electrophilic sites.

Quick check

If a neutral molecule has atoms with FCs that sum to +1, what’s wrong?

The sum must equal the overall charge. A neutral molecule must sum to 0; if you get +1, the Lewis structure or electron count is probably wrong.

3. Step-by-step procedure

  1. Draw a Lewis structure (count total valence electrons first).
  2. For each atom: find V (valence electrons for that free atom).
  3. Count N = nonbonding electrons (lone pairs on that atom).
  4. Count B = total bonding electrons around that atom (each bond = 2 electrons).
  5. Compute FC = V − (N + B/2).
  6. Verify the sum of all FCs equals the molecule/ion charge.

Practice: CO2 (O=C=O)

Find FC for C and each O in the linear CO2 drawn as O=C=O.

Carbon: V=4, N=0, B=8 → FC = 4 − (0 + 8/2) = 0.
Each oxygen (double-bonded): V=6, N=4, B=4 → FC = 6 − (4 + 4/2) = 0.
All atoms have FC = 0 and total = 0 (neutral molecule).

4. Examples

Example A: Ammonium, NH4+

What are the formal charges on N and H in NH4+?

Nitrogen: V=5, N=0, B=8 → FC = 5 − (0 + 8/2) = +1.
Each hydrogen: V=1, N=0, B=2 → FC = 1 − (0 + 2/2) = 0.
Sum = +1, matching the ion's overall charge.

Example B: Methyl cation, CH3+ (challenge)

Compute FC on carbon in CH3+ (structure: carbon bonded to three H, no lone pairs).

Carbon: V=4, N=0, B=6 → FC = 4 − (0 + 6/2) = 4 − 3 = +1.
Each H: FC = 0. Sum = +1 consistent with CH3+.

Example C: Hydrogen cyanide, H–C≡N

Assign FC for H, C, and N in HCN (structure: H–C≡N).

Hydrogen: V=1, N=0, B=2 → FC = 0.
Carbon: V=4, N=0, B=8 (triple bond to N plus single bond to H totals 4 bonds = 8 e⁻) → FC = 4 − (0 + 8/2) = 0.
Nitrogen: V=5, N=2 (one lone pair), B=6 → FC = 5 − (2 + 6/2) = 5 − (2 + 3) = 0.
All atoms FC = 0; structure consistent and neutral.

5. Practice problems (try first, then check answers)

Problem 1

Calculate FCs in NH3 (ammonia). Draw Lewis structure first.

Nitrogen: V=5, N=2 (one lone pair), B=6 → FC = 5 − (2 + 6/2) = 5 − (2 + 3) = 0.
Each H: FC = 0. All FCs are zero for NH3.

Problem 2

Compute FCs for O in H3O+ (hydronium ion).

Oxygen: V=6, N=0 (no lone pairs in H3O+ structure), B=8 → FC = 6 − (0 + 8/2) = 6 − 4 = +2.
However, more commonly hydronium is drawn with one lone pair (N=2) and three bonds (B=6) giving FC = 6 − (2 + 6/2) = 6 − (2 + 3) = +1 — that is the correct representation: O has FC = +1, each H = 0, sum = +1.

Problem 3 (challenge)

For formal-charge practice only: draw CH4, CH3−, and CH3+, then compute FC on carbon in each.

CH4: C: V=4, N=0, B=8 → FC = 4 − 4 = 0.
CH3−: C has an extra lone pair: V=4, N=2, B=6 → FC = 4 − (2 + 3) = −1.
CH3+: (seen above) C FC = +1.

Key Terms & Definitions

TermDefinition
Formal chargeCalculated charge on an atom in a Lewis structure using FC = V − (N + B/2).
Valence electrons (V)Electrons in the outermost shell of the neutral atom used for bonding.
Nonbonding electrons (N)Electrons present as lone pairs on an atom in the Lewis structure.
Bonding electrons (B)Total electrons in bonds around the atom (each bond = 2 electrons).
Hydronium (H3O+)A common ion where formal charge helps explain acidity: O carries a positive formal charge.
Carbocation / CarbanionSpecies with positively or negatively charged carbon centers (FC helps confirm charge location).
