Chapter 2 Summary: Atomic Structure to Nomenclature

This chapter connected atomic theory, bonding principles, molecular shapes, and the foundations of naming organic compounds. Review each section below to ensure full understanding before taking the comprehensive assessment.

1. Review of Atomic Structure

  • Atoms are made up of protons, neutrons, and electrons.
  • Electrons occupy energy levels or shells defined by quantum numbers.
  • Electron configuration determines an element’s chemical behavior.

Checkpoint: Sodium’s Electron Configuration

Write the electron configuration of sodium (atomic number 11).

Answer: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s¹

2. Chemical Bonding Theory

Atoms form bonds to achieve stability, usually a filled outer shell. There are three main bond types:

  • Ionic: Electron transfer between metal and nonmetal.
  • Covalent: Sharing of electron pairs between nonmetals.
  • Metallic: Delocalized “sea of electrons” in metal lattices.

Checkpoint: Bond Identification

Identify the bond type in NaCl, H₂, and Cu.

NaCl → Ionic, H₂ → Covalent, Cu → Metallic.

3. Hybridization and Bonding Geometry

  • sp³: 4 sigma bonds, tetrahedral (e.g., methane).
  • sp²: 3 sigma + 1 pi bond, trigonal planar (e.g., ethene).
  • sp: 2 sigma + 2 pi bonds, linear (e.g., ethyne).

Checkpoint: Hybridization Practice

Predict the hybridization of carbon in ethene (C₂H₄).

Answer: sp² — each carbon forms 3 sigma bonds and 1 pi bond.

4. Drawing Chemical Structures

  • Use Lewis structures to represent valence electrons.
  • Each bond = shared electron pair; each lone pair = unshared electrons.
  • VSEPR theory predicts shape based on electron pair repulsion.

Checkpoint: Water Geometry

What is the shape of H₂O based on VSEPR theory?

Answer: Bent (~104.5°) due to two lone pairs on oxygen.

5. Organic Nomenclature Basics

Nomenclature follows a structured IUPAC system:

  • Prefix – identifies substituents.
  • Parent – longest carbon chain.
  • Suffix – identifies the main functional group.
  • Number to give lowest locants and list substituents alphabetically.

Checkpoint: Structure of IUPAC Name

What is the correct order in a standard IUPAC name?

Answer: Prefix + Parent + Suffix
