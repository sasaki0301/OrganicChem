Chapter 2 Summary: Atomic Structure to Nomenclature

This chapter connected concepts from atomic structure all the way to organic nomenclature. Review each topic below to reinforce your understanding before taking the comprehensive assessment.

1. Review of Atomic Structure

  • Atoms consist of protons, neutrons, and electrons.
  • Electrons are arranged in shells, subshells, and orbitals.
  • Electron configuration determines reactivity and bonding behavior.

Checkpoint: Sodium’s Electron Configuration

Write the electron configuration of sodium (Z = 11).

Answer: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s¹

2. Chemical Bonding Theory

Atoms bond to achieve stable, lower-energy electron configurations.

  • Ionic bonds: electron transfer between metal & nonmetal.
  • Covalent bonds: electron sharing between nonmetals.
  • Metallic bonds: delocalized “electron sea” among metals.

Checkpoint: Bond Identification

Identify the bond type in NaCl, H₂, and Cu.

NaCl → Ionic
H₂ → Covalent
Cu → Metallic

3. Hybridization & Molecular Geometry

  • sp³: 4 sigma bonds → tetrahedral (≈109.5°)
  • sp²: 3 sigma + 1 pi → trigonal planar (120°)
  • sp: 2 sigma + 2 pi → linear (180°)

Checkpoint: Hybridization Practice

Predict the hybridization of each carbon in ethene (C₂H₄).

Answer: Both carbons are sp²-hybridized.

4. Drawing Chemical Structures

  • Lewis structures show valence electrons & bonding pairs.
  • VSEPR predicts 3D geometry by minimizing electron pair repulsion.
  • Bent, trigonal planar, tetrahedral, and linear are common shapes.

Checkpoint: Water Geometry

What is the shape of H₂O based on VSEPR theory?

Answer: Bent (~104.5°) due to two lone pairs on oxygen.

5. Organic Nomenclature Essentials

IUPAC naming consists of three main parts:

  • Prefix – substituents (with locants)
  • Parent – longest carbon chain (root)
  • Suffix – main functional group
  • Number to give the lowest locants.
  • Alphabetize substituents (ignoring di-, tri-, sec-, tert-).

Checkpoint: Structure of an IUPAC Name

What is the correct order?

Answer: Prefix + Parent + Suffix
