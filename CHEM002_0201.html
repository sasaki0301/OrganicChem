Review of Atomic Structure

Understanding atomic structure is essential for studying organic chemistry. Atoms form all matter, and the way electrons are arranged around the nucleus explains bonding, reactions, and molecular behavior.

2. Atomic Number, Mass Number, and Isotopes

- Atomic number (Z) = number of protons
- Mass number (A) = protons + neutrons
- Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons

Activity 2 ‚Äî Identifying Isotopes

Why are Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 considered isotopes of the same element?

Both have 6 protons (so both are carbon), but Carbon-12 has 6 neutrons while Carbon-14 has 8 neutrons.

Key Takeaways

  • Atoms consist of protons, neutrons, and electrons.
  • Atomic number identifies an element; mass number counts protons + neutrons.
  • Isotopes differ in number of neutrons.
  • Electron configuration determines bonding and chemical behavior.
