Cycloalkynes

Cycloalkynes are hydrocarbons that contain a carbon–carbon triple bond within a ring. Unlike cycloalkanes and cycloalkenes, cycloalkynes exhibit significant geometric strain, making them less common and more reactive.

1. What Are Cycloalkynes?

A cycloalkyne is a cyclic hydrocarbon containing at least one C≡C triple bond. Because a triple bond prefers a linear geometry (180°), placing it inside a ring creates strain.

  • Contain a ring (cyclic)
  • Contain at least one triple bond
  • Are unsaturated hydrocarbons

Cyclooctyne — one of the smallest stable cycloalkynes.

2. Geometry of Triple Bonds

Carbon atoms involved in a triple bond are sp-hybridized and prefer a linear geometry (180°).

In rings, this linear geometry cannot be achieved easily, leading to angle strain.

Comparison of linear alkynes vs bent triple bond in a ring.

Activity 1: Geometry Check

Why is a triple bond difficult to place inside a small ring?

Triple bonds prefer a 180° bond angle. Small rings force carbons to bend, creating strong angle strain and instability.

3. Ring Strain in Cycloalkynes

Ring strain increases as ring size decreases. For cycloalkynes, this effect is especially severe due to the rigidity of triple bonds.

  • Small rings → very high strain
  • Large rings → reduced strain
  • Most stable cycloalkynes have ≥ 8 carbons

Smaller rings force severe bending of triple bonds.

Activity 2: Stability Ranking

Which is more stable: cyclopentyne or cyclooctyne?

Cyclooctyne is more stable because its larger ring reduces angle strain.

4. Naming Cycloalkynes (Basic Rules)

Cycloalkynes are named similarly to cycloalkenes, but with the suffix -yne.

  1. Identify the ring size → cyclo-
  2. Locate the triple bond
  3. Number the ring to give the triple bond the lowest locant
  4. Apply substituent rules if present

Example: cyclohexyne (triple bond assumed at C-1).

5. Reactivity of Cycloalkynes

Due to their high strain, cycloalkynes are generally more reactive than linear alkynes. They readily undergo reactions that relieve ring strain.

  • High energy molecules
  • React quickly with electrophiles
  • Used in specialized organic synthesis

Activity 4: Concept Check

Why are cycloalkynes usually more reactive than acyclic alkynes?

Cycloalkynes contain angle strain due to bent triple bonds. Reactions that relieve this strain are energetically favored.
